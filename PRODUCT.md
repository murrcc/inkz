# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two distinct visitors with very different intents land on inkz.io:

1. **Post-meeting Googler.** Anton met them in person (a walk-in, a coffee, a peer introduction) and handed over a card. They go home, Google "INKZ Cairns", land on this site. They want to know: *is this person credible enough to share my business data with?* They're already warm. The site has to hold up under serious scrutiny because the next step is a $50–150k engagement.

2. **Cold visitor + Whisper completer.** Found INKZ via search or word-of-mouth, never met Anton. They land on the site, read the wedge, see the methodology, file a "Preliminary Statement" (the Whisper quiz). They receive an 80–120 word AI-generated forensic finding signed by Anton. If intrigued, they email anton@inkz.io. The site has to do all the credibility work that the in-person walk-in would otherwise do.

The job-to-be-done across both: **decide whether INKZ deserves a real conversation about your business.**

## Product Purpose

INKZ is a forensic audit practice for small-business revenue. Free diagnostic audits identify where revenue is leaking; bespoke $50–150k engagements fix the leaks. inkz.io is the credibility artifact + soft inbound funnel.

Success looks like:
- Post-meeting Googlers email Anton to book the coffee
- Cold Whisper-completers email Anton intrigued
- The site itself reads as a sample of the work product (design IS proof of craft)
- Zero "AI slop" cues. Passes the category-reflex check at both first- and second-order altitudes.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **forensic. restrained. authoritative.**

Voice rules:
- Direct. No hedging. No marketing fluff.
- Plain English. Explain jargon when used.
- Numbers first, opinions second.
- Hedge appropriately on what's not yet known. Acknowledge the limits.
- Confident without being cocky.
- Honest about what INKZ doesn't know yet.

Forbidden words: *drive, leverage, ROI, 10x, synergy, growth hacking.*
Forbidden punctuation: **em dashes (—)**. Use commas, colons, semicolons, periods, or parentheses.

Emotional register: the feeling of opening a federal case file. Deliberate, careful, factual. Not exciting. Not friendly. *Credible.*

## Anti-references

**Specific bad-example aesthetics to escape:**

- **The cream + terracotta + serif "premium consulting" aesthetic.** Fraunces display + Inter body + #faf7f1 background + #b85c38 accent. This is the 2026 first-order AI-design reflex for premium consulting. Saturated in training data. Anton's original DCS proposal used this — the site is intentionally repositioning away from it.
- **Marketing-agency aesthetics.** Bold gradient hero, "we drive growth" copy, big-promise headlines, founder-headshot grids. We are explicitly NOT a marketing agency.
- **SaaS card grids.** Identical card components repeated three or four times. Icon + heading + paragraph. Generic.
- **Hero-metric templates.** Big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent.
- **Glassmorphism.** Frosted-glass cards used decoratively.
- **Gradient text.** `background-clip: text` on a colored gradient.
- **Side-stripe borders.** `border-left: 3px` colored accent on cards/callouts.
- **Webflow/Framer template feel.** Pre-packaged "premium" templates from those ecosystems.
- **The dark + neon "futuristic SaaS" reflex.** Cyberpunk gradients, glowing buttons. Never.
- **Cold corporate blue.** Anything that reads as bank, insurance, or B2B SaaS chrome.

The site lives in a deliberately narrow aesthetic lane: **Forensic Document.** Black on bone-white. Mono captions. Page-corner file markers. Redaction-style reveals. One single saturated red used only for redactions and file stamps.

## Design Principles

1. **The medium is the metaphor.** The site IS a forensic document, not a website that talks about forensic work. Page-corner markers, file numbers, intake forms, exhibit labels. The design and the offer share a vocabulary.

2. **Premium-by-restraint.** Confidence comes from what's removed, not what's added. Real margins. Real whitespace. Mono captions where elaboration would be cheap. No card shadows. No decorative gradients. Hairlines over containers.

3. **Numbers first, opinions second.** When data exists, lead with it. When it doesn't, hedge clearly. The Whisper quiz follows this discipline — ballpark dollar ranges, never false precision.

4. **The forensic accent is sacred.** One saturated red. Used only for redactions, file-stamp ink, and the wedge line. Never on hover states. Never on buttons. Never decoratively. When the red shows up, it means something.

5. **Pass the AI-slop test at two altitudes.** First-order: the aesthetic should not be guessable from the category alone ("forensic accountant → tax-form beige"). Second-order: avoid the obvious anti-reflex too ("forensic but not beige → cyberpunk neon black"). Land somewhere that feels intentional and specific.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast across all text combinations
- Keyboard navigation works everywhere (the file-tab scroll indicator, the modal, the form)
- Screen-reader-friendly: real semantic HTML, mono treatments are decorative not informational
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected fully: cinematic hero collapses to held title card; choreographed scrolls collapse to instant reveals; cursor-proximity effects disabled
- No flashing or strobe effects (the redaction reveals are slow enough to not trigger)
- Forensic-red accent has sufficient contrast against bone background (target 4.5:1+ on all uses)
